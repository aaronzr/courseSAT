Here is a list of requirements extracted from the MSCS Program Sheet (2022-23) for the Artificial Intelligence Specialization:

### General Instructions:
1. Complete a program sheet by filling in the number, name, and units of each course intended for the degree.
2. Submit program sheets and foundation course waiver forms electronically on the Gates Information Network.

### Foundations Requirement:
1. Satisfy the requirements listed in each of the following areas:
   - Logic, Automata & Complexity (CS103)
   - Probability (CS109, Stat116, CME106, MS&E220, or EE178)
   - Algorithmic Analysis (CS161)
   - Computer Organ & Sys (CS107 or 107E)
   - Principles of Computer Systems (CS110 or CS111)
2. Any courses taken elsewhere must be approved by an adviser.
3. Total units used to satisfy the Foundations Requirement must not exceed 10 units.

### Significant Implementation Requirement:
1. At least one course designated as satisfying the Significant Implementation Requirement.
2. Must be taken for a letter grade at Stanford.
3. Courses acceptable for this requirement include: CS 140, 140E, 143, 144, 145, 148, 151, 190, 210B, 212, 221, 227B, 231N, 243, 248/248A, and 341.

### Breadth Requirement:
1. Three courses, each from a different Breadth Area (A, B, C, or D), each taken for at least 3 units and for a letter grade.
2. Breadth Areas and acceptable courses:
   - Area A. Mathematical and Theoretical Foundations: CS 154, 157, 168, 254, 261, 265; EE 364A, 364B; Phil 251
   - Area B. Computer Systems: CS 140, 140E, 143, 144, 149, 212, 242, 243, 244, 244B, 295, 316, 358; EE 180, 282, 382E
   - Area C. Applications: CS 145, 147, 148, 155, 173, 221, 223A, 224N, 224U, 224W, 227B, 228, 229, 229M, 231A, 231N, 234, 236, 237A, 245, 246, 247 (any suffix), 248/248A, 251, 255, 273A, 273B, 279, 345, 347, 348A, 348B, 348C, 348E, 348I, 348K, 355, 356, 373
   - Area D. Computing and Society: CS 152, 181, 182, 256, 281, 329T, 384; AMSTUD 133, 145; ANTHRO 132D; COMM 118S, 120W, 124, 130D, 145, 154, 166, 186W, 230A, 230B, 230C; DESINST 215, 240; EARTHSYS 213; ENGLISH 184D, ENGR 248; HISTORY 244F; INTLPOL 268; LAW 4039; ME 177; MS&E 193, 231, 234, 254; POLISCI 150A; PSYCH 215; PUBLPOL 103F, 353B

### Artificial Intelligence Depth:
1. All depth courses must be taken for a letter grade for 3 or more units.
2. A maximum of 6 units of CS 399 Independent Study may be counted towards the depth.
3. Courses for depth requirement:
   - a) CS 221
   - b) At least four of: CS 223A, 224N, 224S, 224U, 224V, 224W, 228, 229, 231A, 231N, 234, 237A, 237B, 238
   - c) A total of at least 21 units from categories (a), (b) and the following: CS 205L, 224R, 225A, 227B, 229M, 230, 233, 235, 236, 239, 246, 257, 270, 271, 273A, 273B, 274, 275, 279, 281, 322, 324, 325B, 326, 327A, 329 (any suffix), 330, 331B, 332, 333, 345, 348N, 361, 368, 371, 375, 377 (any suffix), 379 (any suffix), 398, 399, 428A, 428B, 432; EE 263, 276, 278, 364A, 364B, 377, 378B; ENGR 205, 209A; MS&E 226, 252; PSYCH 209; STATS 202, 315A, 315B.

### Electives:
1. List additional courses used to complete the 45-unit requirement.
2. Up to 3 units of 1-2 unit seminars offered in the School of Engineering can be counted.
3. All other electives must be taken for 3 or more units.
4. CS courses numbered above 111, excluding CS196, CS198, and CS390A/B/C, can be used as MS electives.
5. Non-CS courses must be technical, numbered above 100, related to the degree program, and approved by the adviser and the MS program administrator.

### Additional Requirements:
1. All courses submitted for the MSCS degree must be numbered 100 or greater.
2. At most 10 units of Foundations requirement courses may be counted toward the 45 units.
3. At most 3 units of 1-2 unit seminars may be counted toward the 45 units.
4. At least 36 units submitted for the MSCS degree, including all courses taken for breadth and depth, must be taken for a letter grade.
5. The average grade in the courses submitted for the MSCS must be at least a B (3.0 in Stanfordâ€™s GPA scale).
6. Units applied toward BS requirements may not also be counted toward the MSCS.
7. Complete at least 45 graduate units at Stanford before receiving the MSCS degree.
